High School High Tech Graduate

Earns ﻿College Scholarship

Leanna Waller, a graduate of the High School High Tech (HSHT) program at our affiliate Abilities of Florida, is studying business management and marine biology at Pasco-Hernando State College, supported by a scholarship she earned through HSHT.


During her time in HSHT, Leanna demonstrated exceptional leadership and commitment, serving as secretary on the HSHT Leadership Council and opportunities supported by the program, including earning her Professional Association of Diving Instructors certification through an HSHT-supported summer internship.

Employee Spotlight:

Hilary Anderson Takes the Plunge ﻿for Special Olympics

ServiceSource Assistant Program Manager Hilary Anderson recently joined employees and their families at the 2025 Special Olympics Virginia Polar Plunge in Fairfax, where she took the plunge and helped raise more than $107,000 to support over 20,000 athletes.


﻿As part of ServiceSource Employment Readiness Program team, Hilary helps individuals prepare for meaningful employment and is known for going the extra mile in all she does.


"Hilary is a huge asset to ServiceSource, and we wanted to give her a lot of credit for her support for our community at the Plunge," said Susan Lambert, whose daughter Emily has worked with Hilary.

Supporting Veteran Career Aspirations

Felicia Riggins, a participant in the Warrior Bridge program for veterans with disabilities at our North Carolina affiliate, Employment Source, is one step closer to launching her own food truck business, thanks to an individual assistance grant awarded through the Myron J. Pasz Memorial Scholarship.


The scholarship, which supports veterans and their immediate family members, was created by the Washkewicz Family Foundation to honor World War II veteran Myron J. Pasz, grandfather of ServiceSource Foundation Trustee Tiffany Washkewicz.

Crabtree Family Supports the ServiceSource Foundation﻿

Ellie Crabtree, wife of ServiceSource Executive Vice President of Operations Larry Crabtree, who passed away in October, presented a check in his memory to the ServiceSource Foundation to support critical programs. After a distinguished career in the Army, Larry served 25 years with ServiceSource, leading multiple food service contracts and operations.


“Larry’s work at ServiceSource meant so much to him on a personal level," Ellie said. "After his years of military service, Larry truly found so much meaning in this second career."


"Larry understood the critical role that the ServiceSource Foundation plays in making sure that people who have disabilities have the programs and supports they need to succeed and thrive! Supporting the Foundation is what Larry would have wanted and I am grateful that we can do this.”
